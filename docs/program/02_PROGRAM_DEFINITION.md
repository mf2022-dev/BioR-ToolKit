# L1-02: Program Definition — What the National Biodefense Program Does

> **Document ID**: L1-02  
> **Level**: 1 — Vision  
> **Audience**: National Decision-Makers  
> **Version**: 1.0  
> **Date**: March 21, 2026  

---

## The One-Sentence Definition

> **The National Biodefense Program builds and operates the national capability to detect, characterize, assess, and respond to biological threats — by connecting the people, laboratories, data, and decision-making systems that currently exist in isolation.**

---

## The 5 Core Functions

Every established national biodefense program in the world performs the same 5 functions. These are not features of a software system — they are **operational capabilities** that a nation either has or doesn't have.

```
DETECT ──→ CHARACTERIZE ──→ ASSESS ──→ RESPOND
                                          │
                    SUSTAIN ◄─────────────┘
                 (keeps the cycle running)
```

---

### Function 1 — DETECT

**"Know that something is happening."**

| What it does | Why it's needed |
|:---|:---|
| Continuous disease surveillance across human, animal, and environmental health | You cannot respond to what you cannot see |
| Early warning system that detects unusual patterns before they become outbreaks | Late detection costs lives and trillions — COVID-19 proved this |
| Integration of hospital data, laboratory results, syndromic surveillance, and community reporting | These data streams exist today but are not connected |
| Environmental and genomic monitoring for emerging pathogens | AMR and novel pathogens don't announce themselves through clinical channels alone |

| Current State (KSA) | Program Delivers |
|:---|:---|
| MoH disease reporting, HESN, individual hospital surveillance, veterinary monitoring — **not integrated** | Unified detection layer that sees across all sectors in near-real-time |

---

### Function 2 — CHARACTERIZE

**"Know exactly what it is."**

| What it does | Why it's needed |
|:---|:---|
| National laboratory network with standardized diagnostic and genomic sequencing capability | Identify the pathogen, its strain, resistance profile, and origin |
| Whole-genome sequencing and phylogenetic analysis for outbreak tracking | Sequence data reveals where it came from, how it spreads, and whether it's engineered |
| Antimicrobial resistance profiling across all clinical and environmental isolates | AMR is a silent pandemic — 1.27M deaths/year, growing in the Gulf region |
| Sample tracking and chain of custody from collection to characterization | Without traceability, lab results cannot support legal or policy decisions |
| Quality management (ISO 15189, ISO 35001) across all participating laboratories | International credibility requires standardized, accredited processes |

| Current State (KSA) | Program Delivers |
|:---|:---|
| Individual reference labs, some sequencing, hospital-level microbiology — **no national LIMS** | Connected national laboratory network with unified sample tracking |

---

### Function 3 — ASSESS

**"Know what it means and how serious it is."**

| What it does | Why it's needed |
|:---|:---|
| Risk assessment framework evaluating events by severity, spread potential, and strategic impact | Not every detection is a crisis. Decision-makers need a standardized severity scale |
| Threat intelligence — monitoring global biological events, publications, and CBRN indicators | Threats don't start at the border. Early awareness gives preparation time |
| Geospatial intelligence — mapping where threats, capabilities, and vulnerabilities overlap | Risk depends on context — location, population density, capability proximity |
| Benchmarking national capability against international standards and peer nations | You need to know where you stand relative to WHO JEE, IHR, GHS Index, and peers |

| Current State (KSA) | Program Delivers |
|:---|:---|
| Scattered risk assessments, WHO JEE participation, academic research — **no continuous assessment** | Standing analytical capability that continuously evaluates biological risk to the Kingdom |

---

### Function 4 — RESPOND

**"Act on what you know."**

| What it does | Why it's needed |
|:---|:---|
| Coordinated response protocols across health, defense, agriculture, and law enforcement | When an event happens, "who does what?" must be pre-defined, not improvised |
| Medical countermeasure (MCM) readiness — stockpiles, distribution plans, surge capacity | Vaccines, antivirals, and PPE must be available before the crisis, not ordered during it |
| Biosecurity enforcement for select agents, dual-use research, and high-containment facilities | Preventing release is cheaper than responding to one |
| Communication and decision-support for national leadership | Decision-makers need clear, timely, actionable information — not raw data |

| Current State (KSA) | Program Delivers |
|:---|:---|
| MoH outbreak response, SCDC, military CBRN units — **no unified coordination** | Coordination layer connecting existing response assets under one operational framework |

> **The program does not replace ministries. It connects them.**

---

### Function 5 — SUSTAIN

**"Keep the capability alive and improving."**

| What it does | Why it's needed |
|:---|:---|
| Workforce development — training biodefense professionals, lab scientists, epidemiologists | Capability without trained people is just equipment in a room |
| Standards and regulatory framework — biosafety, biosecurity, dual-use oversight | International compliance (BWC, IHR, Nagoya Protocol) requires governance structure |
| Research and development — advancing diagnostics, therapeutics, and detection technologies | Threats evolve. Capability must evolve with them |
| International partnerships — WHO, OIE, NATO CBRN COE, bilateral cooperation | No nation does biodefense alone. Intelligence and capability sharing is essential |
| Continuous evaluation and benchmarking | What you don't measure, you can't improve |

| Current State (KSA) | Program Delivers |
|:---|:---|
| Individual training programs, some partnerships, regulatory bodies — **not coordinated** | Institutional structure ensuring the other 4 functions don't decay over time |

---

## Summary: 5 Functions at a Glance

| Function | Question It Answers | Current State | Program Delivers |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Detect** | Is something happening? | Fragmented surveillance | Unified detection |
| **Characterize** | What exactly is it? | Individual labs, no national LIMS | Connected laboratory network |
| **Assess** | How serious is it? | Ad hoc risk assessment | Continuous threat assessment |
| **Respond** | What do we do? | Ministry-level response | Coordinated national response |
| **Sustain** | Can we keep doing this? | No unified program | Workforce, standards, R&D, partnerships |

## How the Prototype (BioR) Maps to the 5 Functions

A working technology prototype has been built to demonstrate that these functions can be integrated into a single platform:

| Function | BioR Component | What It Proves |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Detect | Surveillance dashboard, Early Warning System, SSE real-time notifications | Real-time detection integration is technically feasible |
| Characterize | SampleTrack — 62 LIMS benchmarked, specimen tracking, chain of custody, AMR profiling | National LIMS integration is achievable |
| Assess | Analytics engine (risk scoring, Rt estimation, anomaly detection, forecasting), GeoIntel (489 features across 10 layers), PSEF Benchmark (189 platforms), RSKB (regulatory knowledge base) | Risk assessment and benchmarking can be systematized |
| Respond | Alert management, report generation, MCM tracking, CBRN module | Response coordination can be digitized |
| Sustain | Standards mapping (22 international standards), technology roadmap (28 areas), reference library (24 documents) | A standards and capability baseline exists to measure progress |

> **The prototype is not the program. It is evidence that the program's technology layer is feasible.**

## What the Program Is NOT

| It is NOT | It IS |
|:---|:---|
| A website | A national capability supported by technology |
| A replacement for MoH, SCDC, or military CBRN | A coordination layer connecting existing institutions |
| An academic research project | An operational program with defined functions and deliverables |
| A one-time purchase | A standing capability that requires sustained investment |
| Optional for a nation with Saudi Arabia's risk profile | A strategic necessity aligned with Vision 2030 |
